NEET 2027 Exam Pattern, Syllabus and Preparation Strategy

By the NEETAspirant Team · June 2026 · 8 min read

If you are preparing for NEET 2027, you have roughly 12–14 months from now. This guide covers the syllabus, expected exam pattern, high-weightage topics, and a month-by-month strategy to go from where you are today to exam day.

NEET 2027 Exam Pattern

NEET UG follows a consistent pattern. Based on NTA guidelines and recent years:

  • Total marks: 720
  • Total questions: 180 (each worth 4 marks, with -1 for wrong answers)
  • Sections: Physics (45 questions), Chemistry (45 questions), Biology — Botany (45) + Zoology (45)
  • Duration: 3 hours 20 minutes
  • Mode: Pen and paper (OMR sheet)

Syllabus Overview

NEET syllabus covers Class 11 and Class 12 topics from Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (NCERT-based). NTA has standardised it as follows:

Physics — High Weightage Topics

  • Laws of Motion and Work-Energy (Class 11)
  • Electrostatics and Current Electricity (Class 12)
  • Optics — both Ray and Wave (Class 12)
  • Modern Physics — Atoms, Nuclei, Semiconductors (Class 12)
  • Thermodynamics and Kinetic Theory (Class 11)

Chemistry — High Weightage Topics

  • Organic Chemistry — Class 12 Chapters (aldehydes, amines, polymers)
  • Coordination Compounds (Class 12 Inorganic)
  • Chemical Bonding and Molecular Structure (Class 11)
  • Electrochemistry and Chemical Kinetics (Class 12)
  • p-Block Elements (Class 12)

Biology — High Weightage Topics

  • Genetics and Evolution (Class 12) — consistently 13–15 questions
  • Plant Physiology (Class 11)
  • Human Physiology (Class 11)
  • Reproduction (Class 12)
  • Ecology and Environment (Class 12)
  • Cell Biology and Biomolecules (Class 11)

Month-by-Month Strategy for NEET 2027

July–September 2026: Foundation

Complete Class 11 syllabus across all three subjects. Focus on NCERT reading for Biology. Start Physics from mechanics. Don't start mock tests yet — this phase is purely for concept building.

October–December 2026: Class 12 Coverage

Cover all Class 12 chapters. Finish Organic Chemistry. Do daily Physics problems. Revise Class 11 Biology NCERT at least once. Start topic-wise practice tests.

January–March 2027: Revision + Mocks

Take full-length mocks every 10 days. Analyse each one carefully. Re-read NCERT for Biology and Inorganic Chemistry. Focus heavily on weak chapters identified from mock performance.

April–May 2027: Final Sprint

Weekly full-length mocks. Daily NCERT revision for Biology. Formula sheets for Physics and Chemistry. No new topics — only consolidation. Sleep 7 hours every night.

Key insight: Students who score 600+ in NEET 2027 will almost certainly have done at least 15 full-length mock tests before exam day, and will have analysed each one properly. The number is less important than the quality of analysis.

Summary

NEET 2027 gives you enough time to build from scratch if you start now. The syllabus has not changed — NCERT remains the foundation. Build your schedule around the four phases above, get mentorship early, and treat mocks as learning tools rather than scorecards.
