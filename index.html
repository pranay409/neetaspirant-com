What Every Aspirant Gets Wrong in Year One

Most students waste the first three months on the wrong things. Here is where your energy actually belongs.

📖

NCERT Is Not Optional

Biology especially is almost entirely NCERT based. Before touching any reference book, read every NCERT chapter properly. Underlining the textbook is not reading it. Read it like you are writing the exam tomorrow.

📊

Physics Needs Daily Practice

One hour of problems per day beats three hours on Sunday. Physics in NEET is not hard once you are regular with it. The students who drop marks here are almost always the ones who kept "leaving it for later."

🧪

Organic Chemistry Changes Everything

If you get Organic right, Chemistry stops being your weak link. Named reactions, mechanisms, and functional group conversions from Class 11 and 12 together account for a large share of the Chemistry section.

🧠

Mock Tests Are Your Real Teacher

Taking ten mocks and learning nothing from them is worse than taking three and analysing each one properly. After every mock, write down which topics you are losing marks in. That list is your actual syllabus.

💬

Mentorship Makes the Difference

The students who improve the fastest are usually the ones who have someone to ask when they are stuck. A good mentor can save you weeks of wrong direction, especially in Physics and Organic Chemistry.

😴

Sleep and Rest Are Part of Prep

Running on five hours every night for six months will not get you to 600 plus. Your brain consolidates memory during sleep. Build a schedule you can actually follow, not one that looks good on paper for two weeks.
